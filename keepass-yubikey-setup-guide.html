Security & Privacy Guide May 2026

Offline Sovereign Security

KeePass + YubiKey + pCloud — Full control of your passwords. No subscriptions. No corporate vaults.

Every password manager breach in history happened because someone else held the keys. This setup means only you hold them — backed by hardware, not trust.

What this is

This is a complete, field-tested security stack built around KeePassXC on PC, KeePassDX on Android, and YubiKey hardware keys for two-factor vault access. Your vault syncs across devices using pCloud — a Swiss-based service with a free tier that mounts as a local drive on your PC and keeps your vault available on your phone without depending on your home PC being on.

This is not a beginner setup. It takes an hour to build correctly. Once it's built, it runs itself. No subscriptions, no cloud password manager middlemen, no breach exposure from someone else's server.

What you need
YubiKey 5C Nano
Your primary key. Lives in your PC USB-C port permanently. Tiny enough that it never needs to be removed. Program Slot 2 with HMAC-SHA1 for KeePass challenge-response.
YubiKey 5C NFC (Backup)
Your backup key. Stored at home in a fixed location. Used for Android NFC tap unlock and backup PC access. Program it as a twin of the primary — same HMAC-SHA1 secret, same Slot 2.
KeePassXC (PC)
Free, open source, offline password manager. This is your vault on PC. Download from keepassxc.org — no account required.
KeePassDX Pro (Android)
The Android companion app. Opens the same .kdbx vault file. Available on Google Play. The Pro version supports YubiKey NFC unlock.
pCloud (Free Tier)
Swiss-based cloud storage. Free tier gives you 10GB. Install the desktop app and it mounts as a local drive on your PC. Create a folder called keepass — your vault lives here and syncs to your phone automatically. No home PC dependency.
KeePassXC-Browser Extension
Browser extension for autofill in Chrome, Edge, Firefox. Connects directly to KeePassXC — no cloud, no relay. Install after KeePassXC is running.
Paper and a pen
Your master password and HMAC-SHA1 secret key go on paper — two copies, two locations. This is non-negotiable. If both YubiKeys are lost and you have no paper backup, the vault is gone forever.
Step 1 — Program your YubiKeys
01
Download YubiKey Manager
Get it from yubico.com/support/download. Install and open it. Plug in your primary YubiKey.
02
Program Slot 2 — HMAC-SHA1
In YubiKey Manager: Applications → OTP → Configure Slot 2 → Challenge-Response → HMAC-SHA1. Generate a secret key. Write this secret key on paper immediately — two copies. This is the only time you will see it. Click Finish.
03
Program your backup key identically
Unplug the primary. Plug in your backup YubiKey. Repeat the exact same process in Slot 2 — enter the same HMAC-SHA1 secret key from your paper. Both keys must be twins or your backup won't open the vault.
Step 2 — Set up pCloud vault sync
01
Create your pCloud account
Go to pcloud.com and sign up. Free tier is 10GB — more than enough for a vault file. Download and install the desktop app. It will mount as a drive on your PC.
02
Create your keepass folder
Inside your pCloud drive, create a folder called keepass. This is where your vault file will live. The folder syncs automatically — any device with pCloud installed and logged in sees the same file in real time.
03
Install pCloud on Android
Install the pCloud app on your phone and log in with the same account. Your keepass folder is now accessible from your phone. KeePassDX will point directly to the vault file here — no home network required, works anywhere.
Wolf Warning
pCloud stores your encrypted vault file — not your master password or YubiKey secret. Even if pCloud were breached, the vault file is useless without both. Swiss jurisdiction means no US subpoena access. That said — do not use pCloud to store your master password or paper backups. That defeats the entire system.
Step 3 — Create and configure your vault
01
Create a new vault in KeePassXC
Open KeePassXC → New Database. Save it directly into your pCloud keepass folder. Name it whatever you want — keep it generic. Set encryption to AES-256, Argon2. These are the defaults — don't change them.
02
Set your master password
Choose a strong master password — minimum 20 characters, mix of everything. Write it on the same paper as your HMAC-SHA1 key. Two copies. Two locations. This is the only password you need to remember.
03
Add YubiKey challenge-response
In the database credentials screen, enable Challenge-Response. Select your YubiKey from the dropdown and choose Slot 2. Now the vault requires both your master password and a physical YubiKey touch to open.
04
Configure KeePassXC settings
Tools → Settings: enable launch on Windows startup, set minimize to tray on close, set auto-lock after 15 minutes of inactivity. The vault stays loaded in the tray — it doesn't close when you click X.
05
Install the browser extension
Install KeePassXC-Browser from your browser's extension store. In KeePassXC → Settings → Browser Integration — enable it. Autofill now works directly from your local vault. No cloud relay. No third party.
Step 4 — Android setup with KeePassDX
01
Open the vault from pCloud
Open KeePassDX → Open existing database → navigate to your pCloud folder → select your vault file. The pCloud app makes this folder appear like local storage. You're opening the live synced file — always current.
02
First unlock — YubiKey NFC tap
Enter your master password, then tap your YubiKey 5C NFC to the back of your phone when prompted. NFC sweet spot varies by phone — usually center-back. Hold the key flat against the phone until it responds. After first unlock, fingerprint or PIN handles subsequent unlocks in the same session.
03
Keep KeePassDX running in background
Do not swipe it away from recent apps. It stays unlocked in the background and autofills passwords in your Android browser and apps. Swipe it away and you'll need to unlock again with master password + NFC.
Critical account priority order

Once your vault is running, register both YubiKeys as FIDO2 passkeys on your critical accounts in this order. Do not skip ahead — if you lose email access, you lose everything that resets through it.


Priority 1 — Primary Email
Your email account controls everything else through password resets. Lock this down first with both YubiKeys as FIDO2 passkeys.
Priority 2 — Domain / DNS Provider
Whoever controls your domains controls where your email and sites point. Register both YubiKeys here immediately after email.
Priority 3 — Your Vault File Backup
Confirm your vault is syncing correctly to pCloud and visible on both PC and phone before moving on.
Priority 4 — Hosting / Code Repositories
GitHub, Cloudflare, any site hosting. Both YubiKeys registered as FIDO2.
Priority 5 — Community Platforms
Discord, YouTube, X, Reddit. Register both keys where FIDO2 is supported. Use TOTP in KeePassXC for platforms that don't support hardware keys.
Lockout recovery
01
Can't open vault — try backup key first
Plug in or tap your backup YubiKey and try unlocking. If the primary key failed or was lost, the backup opens the vault identically — they're programmed as twins.
02
Lost one YubiKey
Use the remaining key immediately. Order a replacement from yubico.com the same day. When it arrives, program it with the HMAC-SHA1 secret key from your paper backup — it becomes a new twin. Register it on all FIDO2 accounts before deregistering the lost key.
03
Lost both YubiKeys
Go to your paper backup. Use the HMAC-SHA1 secret key to program a new YubiKey. This is why the paper backup exists and why it lives in two locations. Without the paper, the vault is permanently inaccessible.
Wolf Warning
If both keys are lost and you have no paper backup, the vault cannot be recovered by anyone — including you. The encryption is not breakable. The paper backup is not optional.
The non-negotiable rules
—
KeePass is the only vault. No cloud password managers. No LastPass, no 1Password, no Bitwarden cloud. Ever.
—
Master password lives on paper only. Two copies. Two locations. Never typed into a device for storage. Never photographed.
—
HMAC-SHA1 secret key is on the same paper. Same two copies. Same two locations. This programs replacement keys if yours are lost.
—
Both YubiKeys are registered on every critical account. One key registered means one key lost = lockout. Both keys registered means redundancy.
—
All vault changes happen on PC only. Phone is read-only. Edit on PC, pCloud syncs to phone automatically.
—
No password is reused across any two accounts. KeePassXC has a built-in password generator. Use it for every account.
—
Suspected breach = full compromise assumed. Change everything. Not just the account that was hit.
